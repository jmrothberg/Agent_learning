Build a Tetris game. Falling-block puzzle: Seven tetromino shapes fall one at a time into a vertical well; the player moves them left/right, rotates them, and soft/hard-drops them. Completed horizontal lines clear and award points (more for multiple lines at once), and the fall speed increases with level. Show the next piece and a ghost preview of where the current piece will land. Controls: ArrowLeft/Right move, ArrowUp rotate, ArrowDown soft drop, Space hard drop, R restart. The game ends when the stack reaches the top. HUD with score, level, and lines cleared. Clean colorful blocks with crisp borders on a dark well.
Build a Snake game. A snake moves continuously on a grid, growing one segment each time it eats food that appears at a random cell; speed increases as it grows. The snake turns with the arrow keys but cannot reverse directly into itself. Controls: Arrow keys steer, R restart. The game ends when the snake hits a wall or its own body. Include a wraparound-walls toggle. HUD with current score (length) and high score. Clean high-resolution rounded snake segments and bright food on a dark grid.
Build a Pong game. Two paddles (player on the left, CPU on the right) move vertically and bounce a ball between them; the ball speeds up over a rally and its bounce angle depends on where it strikes the paddle. The CPU paddle tracks the ball with a slight lag so it stays beatable. Controls: ArrowUp/Down or W/S move the player paddle, R restart. First to 11 points wins; a point is scored when the ball passes the opponent's paddle. A dashed center net and each side's score at the top. Clean crisp white paddles and ball on a black court, high-resolution.
Build a Doom game. First-person raycaster shooter (or three.js): The player walks through a textured maze of walls, floor, and ceiling with mouse-look (pointer-lock) and WASD movement, shooting monsters that approach as billboard sprites always facing the camera. Each monster needs an idle frame and an attack/hit frame; the first-person weapon is a screen-space overlay with idle and muzzle-flash firing frames (the muzzle points UP in the source art). A crosshair sits at screen center and the camera does all the aiming. Controls: WASD move, mouse look, click or Space fire, R restart. Pick up health and ammo; clear all monsters to finish the level, dying at zero health. HUD with health, ammo, and score. Generate VIDEO CUTSCENES for a level-intro establishing shot (corridor or hellscape) and a boss-reveal clip before the final encounter (seeded from key-art stills via image-to-video); muted skippable full-screen overlays, FPS gameplay stays in the raycaster. High-resolution detailed wall textures and monster sprites, transparent backgrounds on the sprites, plus gunfire/explosion SFX and ominous looping music.
Build a Minecraft game. First-person voxel sandbox with three.js: The player explores a 3D world of cube blocks (grass, dirt, stone) on chunked terrain, with mouse-look (pointer-lock) and WASD movement plus space-to-jump under gravity. Left-click breaks the targeted block and right-click places the selected block; raycast from the camera to highlight the block face under the crosshair. A small hotbar selects the block type. Controls: WASD move, Space jump, mouse look, left/right click break/place, number keys select block, R restart. High-resolution blocky voxel textures, a bright sky, and simple ambient lighting. Expose the world state for verification.
