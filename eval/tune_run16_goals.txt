# Deliberately spans sprite animation, tile art, lighting, particles, physics,
# board animation, and strategy UI so failures teach different visual systems.

Build a Centipede game. Fixed shooter: The player controls a blaster at the bottom that moves freely in the lower zone and fires upward. A centipede made of segmented body parts winds down through a field of mushrooms; shooting a middle segment splits it into two, and shooting the head scores most. When a shot hits a body segment it leaves a mushroom. The blaster needs idle and firing frames; the centipede head and body are sprites. Controls: Arrow keys move, Space fire, R restart. Clearing the whole centipede advances the wave; a centipede segment or spider reaching the player loses a life. Score and lives on a HUD. Crisp colorful high-resolution sprites, transparent backgrounds.
Build a Galaga game. Formation shooter: The player ship at the bottom moves left/right and fires upward. Insect-like enemies fly in along curving paths, assemble into a formation at the top, then peel off in diving attack runs while firing at the player. Enemies have a 2-frame wing-flap animation. Controls: ArrowLeft/Right move, Space fire, R restart. Clear the wave to advance; being hit by an enemy or its shot loses a life. HUD with score and lives. Crisp colorful high-resolution sprites on a starfield, transparent backgrounds.
Build a 1942 game. Vertical-scrolling shoot-'em-up: The player flies a fighter plane upward over a continuously scrolling sea; enemy planes fly in formation patterns from the top and fire back, and the player shoots upward and can perform a brief barrel-roll to dodge bullets. The player plane has a banking animation when moving left/right; enemy planes are sprites with a propeller shimmer. Power-ups upgrade the player's gun. Controls: Arrow keys move, Space fire, Shift roll/dodge, R restart. Survive the wave to advance; being hit loses a life. HUD with score and lives. Crisp top-down high-resolution sprites over a scrolling ocean, transparent backgrounds.
Build a holochess game. The number-one deliverable is excellent animation: every legal move visibly walks, hops, or glides square-by-square with tweened draw coordinates; knights use a hop arc, idle pieces breathe, promotion transforms, and check shakes or glows the king. Before a captured monster disappears, play a two-beat lift then slam performance. Each piece type is a distinct fantasy monster silhouette with high-resolution transparent idle, walk/hop, lift, and slam sprites for both sides. Human Light plays a depth-3 negamax CPU Dark; block clicks during CPU thinking and animation. Include legal chess, check/checkmate, castling, promotion, highlights, captures tray, and HUD. Controls: mouse, R restart. Expose state.animating, capturePhase, and moving draw coordinates. Generated PNGs must be drawn with drawImage.
Build a Checkers game. Human Gold/Candy plays a depth-3 negamax CPU Purple/Jelly on an 8x8 pastel board. Pieces are glossy gumdrop and jelly mascots rather than flat disks; kings become crowned lollipops. Generate high-resolution transparent idle and 2-3 frame bounce-hop sprites for both sides plus king crowns. Moves bounce-hop instead of teleporting; jump captures squash-and-stretch before the victim pops in a confetti puff; promotion sparkles. Enforce mandatory jumps, multi-jumps, diagonal dark-square moves, and kings. Block human input while CPU thinks or pieces animate. Controls: mouse, R restart. HUD shows turn, captures, CPU thinking, and promotion. Generated PNGs must be drawn with drawImage.
Build a Fieldrunners-style open-field tower defense game. Towers placed on grass build the maze between one entrance and one exit; enemies BFS-pathfind around towers, invalid wall-off placements are rejected, and in-flight creeps re-path when the maze changes. Include Rapid Gun, Missile, Goo, Mortar, Tesla, and Flame towers; waves escalate through soldiers, bikes, jeeps, tanks, helicopters, and blimps. Generate high-resolution transparent sprites for every tower, enemy, and projectile and draw them with drawImage. Rotating turrets use fixed bases plus pre-drawn head angles, recoil, and muzzle flashes; Tesla and Flame stay upright and draw large procedural beams. Sidebar supports place, upgrade, and sell. Controls: click, Space start wave, R restart. Expose enemies, towers, grid, and path.
Build a Roguelike Dungeon game. Procedurally generate rooms connected by corridors; fog-of-war reveals tiles near the player. Arrow keys move one tile per turn and monsters move after each player step. Bump attack monsters, pick up loot, and descend stairs to deeper floors. HUD: HP, depth, gold. Controls: Arrow keys move/attack, R restart. Expose window.state.map and player. Use colorful high-resolution sprites for hero, monsters, wall/floor tiles, loot, and stairs, with transparent backgrounds and drawImage tiles — no ASCII or Unicode glyphs.
Build a Pinball game. A richly illustrated arcade table has a ball falling under gravity and bouncing off walls, animated bumpers, ramps, targets, and two responsive flippers. Bumpers flash and kick the ball, ramps light in sequence, impacts create particles and floating score text, and the table background, ball, bumpers, and flippers use high-resolution generated art drawn with drawImage. Drain between flippers loses the ball; a plunger relaunches. HUD: score, multiplier, balls left. Controls: Z and / or Left/Right Shift flippers, Space plunger, R restart. Expose window.state.ball and flippers.
Build a Bullet Hell Boss game. The player ship dodges dense, beautiful patterned bullet streams—spirals, expanding rings, rotating flowers, and aimed volleys—in a glowing arena. The player hitbox is a tiny visible core smaller than the sprite. A large animated boss changes appearance and bullet palette at HP phase thresholds; the player shoots back. Add impact flashes, trails, particles, screen shake, and a dramatic phase-transition effect while keeping FPS stable. HUD: score, lives, boss HP bar. Controls: Arrow keys move, Shift focus/slow, Space fire, R restart. Expose bullets[], boss hp, and phase.
Build a Torch Dungeon game. Create a richly illustrated dungeon maze where a warm animated torch-light radius follows the player and everything outside falls into shadow and fog-of-darkness—not a fully visible grid. High-resolution hero, guard, wall, floor, torch, exit, and treasure sprites are drawn with drawImage. Patrolling guards appear only when lit; their visible vision cones are occluded by walls. Add flickering light, ember particles, soft shadows, and brief alarm lighting. Reach the exit unseen to win; being caught loses. Controls: Arrow keys move, R restart. Expose player position, guards, light radius, and alert state.
