Build a Donkey Kong game. Single-screen arcade platformer: A plumber hero climbs a stack of sloped girders connected by ladders to rescue a captured figure at the top, while a big ape rolls barrels down toward him. The hero is a detailed character needing animated poses from idle — idle, run (legs cycling), climb (arms reaching up a ladder), jump (legs tucked mid-leap), and a hit/fall — at least 2 frames from idle for each. Barrels roll and bounce down the girders; jumping over a barrel scores points, touching one loses a life. Controls: ArrowLeft/Right run, ArrowUp/Down climb ladders, Space jump, R restart. Reach the top to win the level; lose all lives to game over. Colorful high-resolution sprites, transparent backgrounds.
Build a Doom game. First-person raycaster shooter (or three.js): The player walks through a textured maze of walls, floor, and ceiling with mouse-look (pointer-lock) and WASD movement, shooting monsters that approach as billboard sprites always facing the camera. Each monster needs an idle frame and an attack/hit frame; the first-person weapon is a screen-space overlay with idle and muzzle-flash firing frames (the muzzle points UP in the source art). A crosshair sits at screen center and the camera does all the aiming. Controls: WASD move, mouse look, click or Space fire, R restart. Pick up health and ammo; clear all monsters to finish the level, dying at zero health. HUD with health, ammo, and score. Generate VIDEO CUTSCENES for a level-intro establishing shot (corridor or hellscape) and a boss-reveal clip before the final encounter (seeded from key-art stills via image-to-video); muted skippable full-screen overlays, FPS gameplay stays in the raycaster. High-resolution detailed wall textures and monster sprites, transparent backgrounds on the sprites, plus gunfire/explosion SFX and ominous looping music.
Build a Pac-Man game. Maze game: A yellow chomping hero navigates a walled maze eating dots; four colored ghosts chase him with simple distinct behaviors. The hero needs an animated chomp (mouth opening and closing, facing the move direction); ghosts are sprites with eyes that look toward their movement. Four power-pellets in the corners turn the ghosts frightened (blue) for a few seconds so the hero can eat them for bonus points. Controls: Arrow keys to steer (queued turns), R restart. Eat every dot to clear the level; a ghost catching the hero when not powered loses a life. HUD shows score and lives. Colorful high-resolution sprites, transparent backgrounds, dark maze walls.
Build a Frogger game. Crossing game: A frog starts at the bottom and must cross a busy multi-lane road (cars and trucks moving at different speeds and directions) then a river (hopping on floating logs and turtles; water is deadly) to reach home slots at the top. The frog hops one grid cell per key press and needs idle and mid-hop (legs extended) frames facing its move direction. Controls: Arrow keys hop, R restart. Filling all home slots wins the level; getting hit by traffic, falling in the water, or running out of time loses a life. HUD with score, lives, and a timer. Colorful high-resolution sprites, transparent backgrounds.

Enemies (grippers and knife-throwers) approach and attack; defeating them all advances toward a boss at the end of the floor. Controls: ArrowLeft/Right walk, ArrowDown crouch, J punch, K kick, R restart. A health bar for the hero; losing all health is game over. HUD with score, health, and a level timer. Generate VIDEO CUTSCENES for a floor-intro establishing shot and a dramatic boss-entrance clip before the end-of-floor fight (seeded from key-art stills via image-to-video); muted skippable overlays, the beat-'em-up combat stays sprite-animated. High-resolution detailed sprites, transparent backgrounds, plus punch/kick SFX and tense looping music.
Build a Missile Command game. Defense game: Enemy missiles streak down from the top toward six cities along the bottom; the player aims a crosshair and fires counter-missiles from a base that explode into expanding blasts, destroying any incoming missiles caught in the radius. Controls: mouse moves the crosshair and click fires, or Arrow keys move the crosshair and Space fires; R restart. Each wave sends more and faster missiles; the round ends when all cities are destroyed. Score per missile intercepted, bonus for surviving cities. HUD with score and remaining cities. Glowing high-resolution explosions and missile trails on a night-sky background.

Build a Joust game. Flap-and-joust game: The player rides a flying ostrich and gains altitude by repeatedly flapping (each press gives an upward impulse against gravity); the goal is to collide with enemy knights on buzzards from ABOVE to defeat them, while avoiding being hit from above yourself. The mount needs a flap animation (wings up/down) and a walk frame. Defeated enemies drop an egg to collect before it hatches. Controls: ArrowLeft/Right move, Space flap, R restart. Clear all enemies to advance the wave; being struck from above or touching the lava loses a life. HUD with score and lives. Colorful high-resolution sprites, platforms over lava, transparent backgrounds.
Build a Checkers game. 8x8 board — PLAYER VS COMPUTER, not hotseat: The human plays the bright Gold/Candy side from the bottom and moves first; the computer plays the cool Purple/Jelly side from the top and replies automatically after every human move. Block human clicks while the CPU is thinking and show a 'CPU thinking…' HUD line. FUN GRAPHICS: treat every piece like a carnival candy mascot, not a flat disk — glossy gumdrop men for regular pieces (round, shiny, with cute faces) and crowned lollipop kings when promoted; the board is a pastel checkerboard with soft gradients and sparkle highlights on legal-move squares. Generate high-resolution transparent sprite art for gold gumdrop idle + hop frames and purple jelly idle + hop frames, plus a king crown overlay frame. pieces BOUNCE-hop to their destination (2-3 hop frames) instead of teleporting; on a jump capture, play a quick squash-and-stretch landing on the captured square before the victim pops away in a tiny confetti puff. Enforce real checkers rules: diagonal moves on dark squares only, mandatory jumps when available, multi-jump chains in one turn, and king promotion at the far rank with a brief sparkle animation. CPU opponent: negamax + alpha-beta depth 3 with a simple eval (man=1, king=3, small bonus for back-rank kings and center control) — NOT random moves or 1-ply greed. Schedule CPU moves with setTimeout(200-400ms). Controls: mouse click to select and move, R restart. HUD: score/captures tray, whose turn, promotion and king-crown announcements. Expose window.state for probes.

Idle pieces get a subtle breathing loop. On capture, play the signature two-beat BEFORE the victim disappears: (1) lift — capturer raises victim overhead, (2) slam — drives victim down; generate dedicated lift and slam poses per piece type. Generate high-resolution transparent sprites for BOTH sides: idle + at least THREE walk/hop frames per type, plus lift and slam poses for capturers. RULES: click select + legal-target commit; full chess including capture, check, checkmate. Controls: mouse click, R restart. HUD: turn, captures tray, check/checkmate, 'CPU thinking…', legal-move highlights.
