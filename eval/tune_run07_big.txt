Build a Donkey Kong game. Single-screen arcade platformer: A plumber hero climbs a stack of sloped girders connected by ladders to rescue a captured figure at the top, while a big ape rolls barrels down toward him. The hero is a detailed character needing animated poses from idle — idle, run (legs cycling), climb (arms reaching up a ladder), jump (legs tucked mid-leap), and a hit/fall — at least 2 frames from idle for each. Barrels roll and bounce down the girders; jumping over a barrel scores points, touching one loses a life. Controls: ArrowLeft/Right run, ArrowUp/Down climb ladders, Space jump, R restart. Reach the top to win the level; lose all lives to game over. Colorful high-resolution sprites, transparent backgrounds. Generated PNGs must be drawn with drawImage (not fillRect placeholders). Barrel hazard loop must keep spawning — do not one-shot spawn.
Build a Pac-Man game. Maze game: A yellow chomping hero navigates a walled maze eating dots; four colored ghosts chase him with simple distinct behaviors. The hero needs an animated chomp (mouth opening and closing, facing the move direction); ghosts are sprites with eyes that look toward their movement. Four power-pellets in the corners turn the ghosts frightened (blue) for a few seconds so the hero can eat them for bonus points. Controls: Arrow keys to steer (queued turns), R restart. Eat every dot to clear the level; a ghost catching the hero when not powered loses a life. HUD shows score and lives. Colorful high-resolution sprites, transparent backgrounds, dark maze walls. MECHANICS (any theme): COPY a compact connected maze template — do NOT hand-author rows from scratch. Use tile coords (tx,ty) for player AND chasers (state.chasers[] or state.enemies[]); derive pixels at draw. Scan maze for corridor spawn cells. Chasers must move every frame with the same wall checks as the player. Power pickup sets state.vulnerableTimer (or per-chaser flee mode) for a few seconds. Expose collectiblesLeft or dotsRemaining on window.state.
Build a Joust game. Flap-and-joust game: The player rides a flying ostrich and gains altitude by repeatedly flapping (each press gives an upward impulse against gravity); the goal is to collide with enemy knights on buzzards from ABOVE to defeat them, while avoiding being hit from above yourself. The mount needs a flap animation (wings up/down) and a walk frame. Defeated enemies drop an egg to collect before it hatches. Controls: ArrowLeft/Right move, Space flap, R restart. Clear all enemies to advance the wave; being struck from above or touching the lava loses a life. HUD with score and lives. Colorful high-resolution sprites, platforms over lava, transparent backgrounds. Generated PNGs → drawImage; platforms over lava; flap gives upward impulse against gravity.
Build a Battlezone game. 2D wireframe vector tank game — NOT three.js, NOT sprites, NOT <assets>: First-person wireframe tank combat: wireframe mountains and a ground grid recede toward a horizon; the player sees their tank body and turret as vector outlines in the lower view; enemy tanks and an occasional flying saucer appear as wireframe shapes on the battlefield and shoot back. Controls: ArrowLeft/Right rotate the turret (or hull), ArrowUp/Down drive forward/back, Space fire a vector projectile, R restart. Destroy enemies for score; a hit on the player costs a life. Include a classic radar/minimap in the corner showing enemy blips. HUD shows score and lives. Clean glowing vector line art on black; expose window.state with player yaw/position and enemy list for probes. ArrowLeft = turn/turret left (decrease yaw); ArrowRight = turn right (increase yaw). Do not swap or invert — reversed controls fail immediately. ArrowUp/Down = drive forward/back.
Build a Star Wars game. 2D wireframe vector trench run — NOT three.js, NOT sprites, NOT <assets>: First-person wireframe flight down a Death Star trench: converging trench walls, tower blocks, and TIE fighter wireframes approach from ahead; a fixed crosshair sits at screen center and the player fires glowing vector bolts. Controls: ArrowLeft/Right (or mouse) steer laterally in the trench, Space fire, R restart. Score points for destroying towers and fighters; reach the exhaust port at the end to win; collision with trench walls or an obstacle loses a life. HUD shows score and lives. Clean glowing vector line art on black; expose window.state with player lateral position, obstacles[], and projectiles for probes. Crosshair moves on both axes: ArrowLeft/Right = lateral; ArrowUp/ArrowDown = vertical aim (crosshair Y or trench pitch). Space fires at crosshair position — not lateral-only steering.
Build a holochess game. 8x8 board (or circular holochess table): THE #1 DELIVERABLE IS EXCELLENT ANIMATION — rules and CPU are required, but a chess game that teleports pieces or holds a single frozen sprite is a failure even if the logic is correct. Every legal move must PLAY OUT as a visible performance: monsters walk, hop, or glide square-by-square along the path (never snap board[] and redraw — tween drawX/drawY and commit board[] only when the animation finishes). While moving, cycle walk frames on a timer (~120ms per frame); knights get a visible hop arc; castling slides king and rook together; pawn promotion plays a brief transform; check makes the king monster shake or glow. Idle pieces get a subtle breathing loop. On capture, play the signature two-beat BEFORE the victim disappears: (1) lift — capturer raises victim overhead, (2) slam — drives victim down; generate dedicated lift and slam poses per piece type. Block all clicks while state.animating; expose state.animating, state.capturePhase, and moving piece draw coords for probes. ART: every piece is a distinct fantasy MONSTER (not Unicode glyphs) with silhouettes that read as pawn/knight/bishop/rook/queen/king — small grunts, leaping beast-riders, tall mystics, fortress-beasts, multi-limbed queens, crowned leaders; light vs dark palettes. Generate high-resolution transparent sprites for BOTH sides: idle + at least THREE walk/hop frames per type, plus lift and slam poses for capturers. Use the SAME character description in every asset prompt (fixed seed) so walk/lift/slam match idle. Flip sprites to face direction of travel. PLAYER VS COMPUTER: human White/Light bottom moves first; computer Black/Dark top replies automatically after each human move — not hotseat, no AI-off default. Block human input during CPU think AND during any piece animation. CPU: negamax + alpha-beta depth 3, material eval (P=1 N/B=3 R=5 Q=9), scheduled with setTimeout(200-400ms). RULES: click select + legal-target commit; full chess including capture, check, checkmate. Controls: mouse click, R restart. HUD: turn, captures tray, check/checkmate, 'CPU thinking…', legal-move highlights. Generated PNGs → drawImage. Tween drawX/drawY; block input while state.animating; lift/slam before capture remove. CPU uses setTimeout(200-400ms).
