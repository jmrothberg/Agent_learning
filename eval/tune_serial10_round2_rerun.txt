Build a Donkey Kong game. Single-screen arcade platformer: A plumber hero climbs a stack of sloped girders connected by ladders to rescue a captured figure at the top, while a big ape rolls barrels down toward him. The hero is a detailed character needing animated poses from idle — idle, run (legs cycling), climb (arms reaching up a ladder), jump (legs tucked mid-leap), and a hit/fall — at least 2 frames from idle for each. Barrels roll and bounce down the girders; jumping over a barrel scores points, touching one loses a life. Controls: ArrowLeft/Right run, ArrowUp/Down climb ladders, Space jump, R restart. Reach the top to win the level; lose all lives to game over. Colorful high-resolution sprites, transparent backgrounds.
Build a Kung-Fu Master game. Side-scrolling beat-'em-up: A martial-artist hero walks left/right through a scrolling stage and fights waves of enemies who close in from both sides. The hero is a detailed character needing animated poses from idle — idle, walk, crouch, punch (arm fully extended), kick (leg raised high), and a hit/knockdown — at least 2 frames from idle for each so the strikes read clearly. Enemies (grippers and knife-throwers) approach and attack; defeating them all advances toward a boss at the end of the floor. Controls: ArrowLeft/Right walk, ArrowDown crouch, J punch, K kick, R restart. A health bar for the hero; losing all health is game over. HUD with score, health, and a level timer. Generate VIDEO CUTSCENES for a floor-intro establishing shot and a dramatic boss-entrance clip before the end-of-floor fight (seeded from key-art stills via image-to-video); muted skippable overlays, the beat-'em-up combat stays sprite-animated. High-resolution detailed sprites, transparent backgrounds, plus punch/kick SFX and tense looping music.
Build a Fieldrunners game. Open-field tower defense: No pre-drawn path: grass field with one entrance and one exit; towers on free tiles BUILD THE MAZE — enemies BFS-pathfind around towers (reject any placement that walls the exit; re-path in-flight creeps when the maze changes). Fieldrunners tower set: Rapid Gun, Missile (splash), Goo (slow), Mortar (long range), Tesla (lightning beam), Flame (short-range splash beam). Enemies escalate by wave: soldiers, bikes, jeeps, tanks, helicopters, blimps. Sidebar: click tower type, click grid to place, click placed tower to upgrade or sell. Kills earn money; leaks cost lives; win after clearing a long campaign (~45 waves). HUD: money, lives, wave. Controls: click, Space start wave, R restart. Generate <assets> sprites for each tower, enemy, and projectile; draw with drawImage. ROTATING TURRETS (gun/missile/goo/mortar): fixed base plus 5 pre-drawn head angles (E/SE/S/NE/N; mirror for west); lerp aim toward target, recoil, muzzle flash or projectile — do NOT ctx.rotate one whole tower sprite. BEAM TOWERS (Tesla/Flame): vertical upright sprite; tower stays fixed; on fire draw a large procedural lightning or flame beam from the tower top to the enemy. Expose window.state with enemies[], towers[], grid, path.
Build a Joust game. Flap-and-joust game: The player rides a flying ostrich and gains altitude by repeatedly flapping (each press gives an upward impulse against gravity); the goal is to collide with enemy knights on buzzards from ABOVE to defeat them, while avoiding being hit from above yourself. The mount needs a flap animation (wings up/down) and a walk frame. Defeated enemies drop an egg to collect before it hatches. Controls: ArrowLeft/Right move, Space flap, R restart. Clear all enemies to advance the wave; being struck from above or touching the lava loses a life. HUD with score and lives. Colorful high-resolution sprites, platforms over lava, transparent backgrounds.
Build a Checkers game. 8x8 board — PLAYER VS COMPUTER, not hotseat: The human plays the bright Gold/Candy side from the bottom and moves first; the computer plays the cool Purple/Jelly side from the top and replies automatically after every human move. Block human clicks while the CPU is thinking and show a 'CPU thinking…' HUD line. FUN GRAPHICS: treat every piece like a carnival candy mascot, not a flat disk — glossy gumdrop men for regular pieces (round, shiny, with cute faces) and crowned lollipop kings when promoted; the board is a pastel checkerboard with soft gradients and sparkle highlights on legal-move squares. Generate high-resolution transparent sprite art for gold gumdrop idle + hop frames and purple jelly idle + hop frames, plus a king crown overlay frame. pieces BOUNCE-hop to their destination (2-3 hop frames) instead of teleporting; on a jump capture, play a quick squash-and-stretch landing on the captured square before the victim pops away in a tiny confetti puff. Enforce real checkers rules: diagonal moves on dark squares only, mandatory jumps when available, multi-jump chains in one turn, and king promotion at the far rank with a brief sparkle animation. CPU opponent: negamax + alpha-beta depth 3 with a simple eval (man=1, king=3, small bonus for back-rank kings and center control) — NOT random moves or 1-ply greed. Schedule CPU moves with setTimeout(200-400ms). Controls: mouse click to select and move, R restart. HUD: score/captures tray, whose turn, promotion and king-crown announcements. Expose window.state for probes.
Build a holochess game. 8x8 board (or circular holochess table): THE #1 DELIVERABLE IS EXCELLENT ANIMATION — rules and CPU are required, but a chess game that teleports pieces or holds a single frozen sprite is a failure even if the logic is correct. Every legal move must PLAY OUT as a visible performance: monsters walk, hop, or glide square-by-square along the path (never snap board[] and redraw — tween drawX/drawY and commit board[] only when the animation finishes). While moving, cycle walk frames on a timer (~120ms per frame); knights get a visible hop arc; castling slides king and rook together; pawn promotion plays a brief transform; check makes the king monster shake or glow. Idle pieces get a subtle breathing loop. On capture, play the signature two-beat BEFORE the victim disappears: (1) lift — capturer raises victim overhead, (2) slam — drives victim down; generate dedicated lift and slam poses per piece type. Block all clicks while state.animating; expose state.animating, state.capturePhase, and moving piece draw coords for probes. ART: every piece is a distinct fantasy MONSTER (not Unicode glyphs) with silhouettes that read as pawn/knight/bishop/rook/queen/king — small grunts, leaping beast-riders, tall mystics, fortress-beasts, multi-limbed queens, crowned leaders; light vs dark palettes. Generate high-resolution transparent sprites for BOTH sides: idle + at least THREE walk/hop frames per type, plus lift and slam poses for capturers. Use the SAME character description in every asset prompt (fixed seed) so walk/lift/slam match idle. Flip sprites to face direction of travel. PLAYER VS COMPUTER: human White/Light bottom moves first; computer Black/Dark top replies automatically after each human move — not hotseat, no AI-off default. Block human input during CPU think AND during any piece animation. CPU: negamax + alpha-beta depth 3, material eval (P=1 N/B=3 R=5 Q=9), scheduled with setTimeout(200-400ms). RULES: click select + legal-target commit; full chess including capture, check, checkmate. Controls: mouse click, R restart. HUD: turn, captures tray, check/checkmate, 'CPU thinking…', legal-move highlights.
